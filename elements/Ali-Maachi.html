Ali Maachi

Ali Maachi (Tiaret, August 12, 1927, June 8, 1958) is an Algerian poet and singer known for his militant songs for the independence of Algeria.

Biography

Ali Maachi was born on August 12, 1927 in Tiaret, in the district of Rass Essouk.

As a child, he abandoned his studies to help his farmer father. In 1949, he did his military service at the Bizerte maritime base in Tunisia.

At the end of his service, he moved to Algiers and worked as a technician at Radio-Alger where he rubbed shoulders with many artists.

He travels a lot and, in 1953, he founded the musical group "Saffir Ettarab" ("Ambassador of the song"). His recital of the Oran genre was committed and dedicated to his homeland Algeria. His first song is El-Babour ("The boat") but it is his song Angham El-Djazaïr ("Melodies of Algeria") from 1956 which is the most famous.

Arrest and assassination

Ali Maâchi fut enlevé par les soldats de l'armée française le 8 juin 1958 avec deux de ses compagnons. Ils furent pendus et leurs corps exhibés sur la place Carnot de la ville de Tiaret
