Tahar BenFarhat

Born on March 23, 1944 in Tiaret, Tahar Benferhat began his career at JSM Tiaret at the age of 18, a club in which he spent his entire professional career as a football player.

A robust defender, he became a starter in the club's first team at the age of 20, and an Algerian international under the orders of Abderrahmane Ibrir, during a match between Algeria and Dinamo Zagreb.

He was captain of the Algerian team in the 1970s. He received a total of 34 caps for the Algerian team, for one goal. In addition, his heavy strike allows him to score a quadruple against GC Mascara

The late Tahar Benferhat enjoyed international recognition in 1972 and 1973 when he was part of the African selection set up by the Confederation of African Football (CAF) to participate in the mini-World Cup hosted by Brazil. . The African selection was driven by Rachid Mekhloufi. The child of Tiaret had a good tournament and he was retained in the standard team of the competition.
